# 🌿 Where I’m Headed

I’m building my way toward a career in Identity and Access Management.

Right now, my goal is to grow into an IAM Analyst role. Long term, I want to move closer to identity architecture: understanding not only how access works, but why it should be designed a certain way in the first place.

I’m interested in the space between people, systems, roles, permissions and real business life. The part where a user changes teams, a migration affects access, old permissions stay behind, a process depends on one forgotten spreadsheet and everyone suddenly wonders why IT is on fire.

That is the space I want to understand better.

This repository is where I collect my learning, notes, experiments and thinking around identity, access, governance and practical IT systems.


---

## 🧭 About This Space

This repository is my living portfolio and learning notebook.

It is not meant to look like a finished product. It is meant to show direction, progress and how I think.

Here I collect:

- **IAM Experiments** – Small practical studies around users, roles, access and identity lifecycle
- **Identity Governance Notes** – Thoughts around IGA, access reviews, approvals, policies and ownership
- **Architecture Thinking** – Diagrams, system logic and notes about how things connect
- **Troubleshooting Reflections** – Lessons from real IT support situations, migrations and broken processes
- **Learning Snapshots** – Notes, unfinished ideas and concepts I want to return to later

The main theme is simple:

How do we make sure the right people have the right access at the right time, without creating chaos for users, IT or security?

## 🧩 Why This Matters To Me

I have seen how important reliable IT really is across very different environments: retail systems, healthcare patient information systems and factory operations.

Retail, healthcare and manufacturing all work differently, but they share one critical dependency: people need the right access to the right systems at the right time.

When that fails, work slows down, customers wait, patient care is affected and production can suffer.

When access is planned badly, users feel it immediately. Data becomes unreachable. Accounts are missing. Old permissions remain. New permissions are wrong. Communication fails. Support gets blamed for decisions they did not make and users lose trust in IT.

**That experience matters**.

I do not want to learn IAM only from diagrams and documentation. I want to connect architecture-level thinking with what actually happens to people when systems change.

That is one of the reasons I am interested in IAM, IGA and identity architecture.

Good identity work is not just security.

It is structure, timing, ownership, communication and clean logic.
